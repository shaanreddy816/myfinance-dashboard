# 🎓 Financial Education & Calculators Feature

## Overview

A comprehensive financial education and planning section has been added to FamLedger AI. This feature helps users understand key financial concepts and provides interactive calculators for making informed financial decisions.

## How to Access

1. Open FamLedger AI (https://famledgerai.com)
2. Look for **"Financial Education"** in the sidebar navigation under "Advanced" section
3. Click to access all educational content and calculators

## Features

### 1. 🛡️ Emergency Fund Education & Calculator

**Learn:**
- What is an emergency fund and why you need one
- When to use it (job loss, medical emergencies, urgent repairs)
- When NOT to use it (vacations, planned purchases)
- Best practices for maintaining your emergency fund

**Calculate:**
- Your exact emergency fund requirement
- Inflation-adjusted future value (8% general + 12% medical inflation)
- Breakdown of general vs medical expenses
- Choose 6, 9, or 12 months coverage

**Example:**
- Monthly Expenses: ₹50,000
- Medical Expenses: ₹10,000
- Duration: 6 months
- Result: Current need ₹3,60,000 → Future value (inflation-adjusted) ₹3,78,000

### 2. 📈 Age-Based SIP Recommendations

Get personalized investment advice based on your age:

**20s - Aggressive Growth**
- 85% Equity, 15% Debt
- Focus: Long-term wealth creation
- Horizon: 15-25 years
- Funds: Large/Mid/Small cap, Index funds

**30s - Balanced Approach**
- 75% Equity, 25% Debt
- Focus: Goal-based investing (home, education)
- Horizon: 10-20 years
- Funds: Large cap, Balanced, Multi-cap, ELSS

**40s - Moderate Risk**
- 55% Equity, 45% Debt
- Focus: Retirement planning
- Horizon: 10-15 years
- Funds: Large cap, Hybrid, Debt, Corporate bonds

**50s+ - Conservative**
- 35% Equity, 65% Debt
- Focus: Capital preservation
- Horizon: 5-10 years
- Funds: Conservative hybrid, Debt, FMPs, SCSS

### 3. 💰 SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) Calculator

**Calculate how much corpus you need for regular monthly income:**

**Example:**
- Want: ₹50,000/month
- Expected Return: 5% annually
- Duration: 10 years
- **Result: Need ₹47,20,000 corpus**

**Features:**
- Year-by-year breakdown showing:
  - Opening balance
  - Withdrawal amount
  - Returns earned
  - Closing balance
- Corpus depletion warning if needed
- Helps plan retirement income

### 4. 💡 Smart Investment Ideas for Passive Income

Explore 7 investment options beyond mutual funds:

1. **REITs** - 7-10% returns, Medium risk, High liquidity
2. **Dividend Stocks** - 4-6% yield, Medium-High risk
3. **Debt Mutual Funds** - 6-8% returns, Low-Medium risk
4. **Fixed Deposits** - 5-7% returns, Very Low risk
5. **NPS** - 8-10% returns, Tax benefits
6. **Rental Income** - 2-4% yield, Real estate
7. **Corporate Bonds** - 7-9% returns, Moderate risk

Each option includes:
- Expected returns
- Risk level
- Liquidity
- Tax implications
- Detailed description

### 5. 💾 Save Your Calculations

- Save any calculation for future reference
- View all saved calculations with timestamps
- Delete calculations you no longer need
- Data persists across sessions (stored in browser)

## Inflation Considerations

The calculators account for inflation:
- **General Inflation**: 8% annually (default, adjustable)
- **Medical Inflation**: 12% annually (default, adjustable)

**Why it matters:**
- ₹1,00,000 today ≠ ₹1,00,000 in 10 years
- After 10 years at 8% inflation, you need ₹2,15,892 for same purchasing power
- Always plan with inflation-adjusted numbers

## Common Questions

**Q: How much emergency fund do I need?**
A: Minimum 6 months of expenses, ideally 12 months. Use the calculator to get your exact number.

**Q: Which age group should I select for SIP?**
A: Select based on your current age. The recommendations adjust risk based on your investment horizon.

**Q: What return rate should I use in SWP calculator?**
A: Conservative: 5-6%, Moderate: 7-8%, Aggressive: 9-10%. Lower is safer for planning.

**Q: Can I save multiple calculations?**
A: Yes! Save as many as you want. They're stored in your browser.

**Q: Do I need to create an account?**
A: No, the feature works without any account. But saved calculations are browser-specific.
